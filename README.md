# Microglial Heterogeneity and SPP1‑Driven Microglia–Astrocyte Crosstalk Orchestrates White‑Matter Neuroinflammation in ALS

## Abastract
### Background
Neuroinflammation is a defining feature of amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS), yet the molecular programs modulating glial interactions in the spinal cord remain incompletely understood. Microglia are increasingly recognized as heterogeneous responders underlying ALS pathogenesis, but the mechanisms linking specific microglial states to astrocytic dysfunction and tissue‑level pathology are not fully unresolved.
### Methods and results
Using single‑cell transcriptomics and spatial transcriptomics of human ALS spinal cord, we identified a striking regional bias in microglial activation, with white matter exhibiting a disproportionately amplified inflammatory response. A distinct SPP1high microglial population emerged as a major contributor to dysregulated glial crosstalk to CD44 expressing astrocytes. SPP1high microglia also display transcriptional signatures of enhancing integrin-related cell adhesion and phagocytosis functions that diverge from canonical homeostatic or disease‑associated microglial programs. Histology analysis across multiple ALS mouse models confirmed spatially patterned microglial activation, and Spp1+ microglia-Cd44+ astrocyte crosstalk was validated in hSOD1G93A mice, which exhibited the strongest Spp1+ microglial response. Functionally, recombinant Spp1 promoted a pro-inflammatory transcriptional drift in astrocyte state, and exacerbated neuroinflammatory pathology in hSOD1G93A mice in vivo.
### Conclusions
These findings identified spinal cord white matter as the vulnerable hub for ALS neuroinflammation and SPP1 as a central regulator of glial crosstalk that directly reprograms astrocytic behaviour and amplifies ALS neuroinflammation. The SPP1–CD44 signalling axis represents a mechanistic driver of ALS pathogenesis and a candidate therapeutic target for selectively attenuating maladaptive glial interactions.

## Key words
Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis, microglia heterogeneity, neuroinflammation, SPP1, CD44, glial crosstalk
